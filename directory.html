Every Sikh MP. One standard.

Six parts of this series showed what happens when Canadians wait for politicians to lead. Nothing happens until someone makes noise. This is the noise, organized. Every sitting Sikh Member of Parliament, their official contact, and four questions they haven't had to answer on the record.

WHY EVERY RIDING

You don't need to live there. A Canadian citizen can write to any MP. Representation isn't a postal code. If an MP claims to speak for Sikh Canadians nationally, every Sikh Canadian gets to ask what that claim is worth.

WHY THESE QUESTIONS

They're checkable. Not opinions. Not gotchas. Public donations, public votes, public safety. Every question here can be verified against a public record, which is exactly why they're hard to dodge.

WHY IT'S TRACKED

Silence is a data point. This page records who answered, who acknowledged, and who said nothing. That record becomes part of the series. Nobody gets to disappear into an inbox.

The Four Questions

  • 1The RCMP named the Government of India from a podium in October 2024. What would it take for you to name it from the floor of the House?
  • 2Your Elections Canada returns are public. Your standards are not. What checks do you run before accepting a donation, and what would make you return one?
  • 3How do you measure whether Sikh constituents in your riding are safer this year than last? What does that measurement show?
  • 4Constituents in ridings across this country have paid for LMIA jobs that did not exist. What have you personally done for them? Not the government. You.
